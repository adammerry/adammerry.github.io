Types of large cats: (hover over pics for more info & videos)

The lion is known as "king of the jungle", and that nickname exists for a reason. These bad boys are known to feed on everything from birds and reptiles to wildebeests, zebras, and even elephants. 10,000 years ago the lion was the most widespread large land mammal after humans, but due to habitat loss and conflicts with humans they are now classified as a vulnerable species.

Lion video

Even though lions may be "king of the jungle", tigers (on average, their sizes can vary widely) are actually the largest living cat. They only naturally exist in Asia, and can have unique color variations from white to orange to gold. Tigers are one of only two big cats that are fond of water, the other being jaguars.

Tiger video

Jaguars are the only non-extinct big cats in the Panthera species (which encompasses lions, tigers, jaguars and leopards) native to the Americas. They are the third largest feline, after lions and tigers. Jaguar's exceptionally strong bite allows them to bite directly through the skull and fatally pierce the brains of their prey.

Jaguar video

Cheetahs have a lighter, more slender body compared to other big cats, which is built for speed. They generally inhabit forests and savannahs in Africa and western Asia. The cheetah is the fastest land animal on earth, and can go from 0 to 60 mph in 3 seconds, with their top speed being around 70 mph.

Cheetah video

The cougar, also known as the mountain lion, panther, puma, or catamount (yeah they're all the same thing) is more closely related to the domestic house cat than any of the Panthera genus (lion, tiger, jaguar, and leopard). Their range extends from the Canadian Yukon to the Southern Andes Mountains. Although large, the cougar is sometimes preyed upon by jaguars, wolves, and bears.
